Structure → Story → Soul: The Mástoras Approach to Business Clarity

By Garry Nicholl • March 26, 2026 • 5 min read

How Mástoras turns unclear business thinking into something you can actually use — the Structure → Story → Soul approach.

Every business has a story to tell. But having a story isn't enough. You need one that makes sense to the person hearing it — clear enough that they understand what you do and why it matters.

That's where our Structure → Story → Soul approach comes in. It's not just a process; it's a philosophy for business communication that we've developed and refined working with founders, tradespeople, and community organisations across Northern Ireland.

The Three Pillars

1. Structure: The Foundation

Before you can tell a compelling story, you need a solid foundation. Structure is about organizing your business thinking — clarifying your value proposition, identifying your target audience, and mapping out your strategic objectives.

We call this the "Clarity Check" — a comprehensive assessment that cuts through the confusion and gives you a clear roadmap for success.

2. Story: The Connection

Once you have structure, it's time to bring it to life. Story is about crafting narratives that resonate emotionally with your audience. Whether it's a brand story, a funding application, or a marketing campaign, we help you transform dry facts into compelling narratives.

This is the "Story Reframe" — taking your business message and presenting it in a way that people can't ignore.

3. Soul: The Impact

The final pillar is soul — the authentic, human element that makes your business memorable. Soul is about connecting with your audience on a deeper level, building trust, and creating lasting relationships.

This is where the work stops sounding like everyone else's, and starts sounding like you.

Why This Approach Works

The approach works because it starts with clarity, not clever words. Get the thinking straight first, and the story tends to tell itself — to funders, to customers, to whoever needs to understand what you're doing.

Whether you're a startup seeking investment, a tradesperson wanting to stand out, or a community organisation applying for funding, this approach helps you explain what you do without losing people along the way.
